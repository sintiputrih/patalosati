Dax Tejera Obituary – What Happened To Him? 

Dax Tejera, 37, died unexpectedly on Friday, December 23. ABC News President Kim Godwin made the surprise news in a statement to the team, expressing her “deep grief and a wounded heart.”

Godwin did vow to release additional information about Dax Tejera’s death in the coming days.

In response to Tejera’s demise, Godwin released the following statement last month: “We regret to inform you that Dax Tejera, a friend, and coworker, passed away unexpectedly after a heart attack yesterday night. As executive producer, Dax’s enthusiasm, love, and energy for ABC News, That Week With George Stephanopoulos, and you sparkled every Sunday morning. His gorgeous girls received the same level of devotion from him. Our thoughts are with Dax’s wife Veronica, their two young daughters, and the Tejera family as a whole.”

Dax Tejera Cause Of Death 

Dax Tejera’s cause of death has officially been made public, and in contrast to what ABC News president Kim Godwin claimed earlier this month, the ABC News producer did not pass away on December 23 due to a heart attack.

According to ET, Tejera choked to death while out to dinner at Bobby Van’s Steakhouse with his wife and friends because he was intoxicated. Tejera died from “asphyxia due to obstruction of the airway by food bolus exacerbating acute alcohol intoxication,” a spokeswoman for the New York City Office of Chief Medical Examiner told ET.

Tejera served as an executive producer for George Stephanopoulos’ This Week. He was 37.

Tejera’s wife, Veronica, was detained shortly after the tragedy on suspicion of abandoning their 2-year-old and 5-month-old girls alone at the Yale Club in the hours preceding Dax’s untimely demise.
