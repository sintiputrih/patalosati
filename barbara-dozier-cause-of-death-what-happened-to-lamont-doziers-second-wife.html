What happened to Lamont Dozier's second wife?

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, January 17, 2024

Lamont Dozier born June 16, 1941, was a Detroit, Michigan-born American singer, songwriter, and record producer.

He co-wrote and produced 14 Billboard number one hits in the United States and four in the United Kingdom.

Barbara Dozier cause of death: What happened to Lamont Dozier’s second wife?

Dozier got married to Barbara in 1980 years after his first marriage to Elizabeth had ended.

The marriage lasted until Barbara passed away in 2021. Their over 40 years love affair yielded great results for the career of Lamont.

Barbara according to sources was a great pillar behind the soul singer. She had 3 children with Lamont before her sad passing.

Dozier co-founded and owned Invictus Records and Hot Wax Records, which produced hits for acts like Freda Payne, Honey Cone, Chairmen of the Board, and 100 Proof Aged in Soul.

He was a member of the team that created most of the Motown sound and numerous hit records by artists like Martha and the Vandellas, The Supremes, and The Isley Brothers.

Dozier was the team’s musical arranger and producer, working alongside Brian Holland.

Many of the Supremes’ early hits, including “Where Did Our Love Go” and “Come and Get These Memories,” were written and produced by the trio, as were Martha and The Vandellas’ “Heatwave” and “Quicksand”.

“Why Can’t We Be Lovers,” his most successful song, peaked at number 9 on Billboard’s R&B chart.

In 1973, Harold Beatty took his place as the new musical arranger when he finally quit the band.
