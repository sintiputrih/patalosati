John Lithgow

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, February 19, 2024

Who is John Lithgow?

John Lithgow is a talented American stage and character actor. He is also a poet, singer, and musician. He is best known for his portrayal of Dick Solomon in the NBC comedy “3rd Rock from the Sun.” In the Netflix original historical drama online television ‘The Crown,’ he played Winston Churchill, the United Kingdom’s first Prime Minister.

What is the Net Worth of John Lithgow? Salary, Earnings

How much money does John Lithgow have? As a result of his many careers as an actor, musician, and author, the actor has amassed a net worth of around $45 million. He earned $75,000 for each episode of the comedic television series 3rd Rock from the Sun during the 1996-1997 season.

Similarly, he was paid $200,000 per episode during the 1999-2000 season. He was the highest-paid actor in The Crown, despite having less screen time than the main character Claire Foy.

Where was John Lithgow born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

John Lithgow was born on October 19, 1945, into a theatrical family in Rochester, New York, USA. John Arthur Lithgow’s full name is John Arthur Lithgow. He is a mixed-race American of Welsh, English, Scots-Irish, Northern Irish, and Scottish ancestry. Arthur Washington Lithgow III (father) and Sarah Jane (mother) are his parents’ names.

His father is a well-known producer and director who previously ran Princeton, New Jersey’s McCarter Theatre. Similarly, his mother was an actress in the past. He also has a younger brother named Robin Lithgow. In Princeton, New Jersey, John Lithgow attended Princeton High School. He earned a bachelor’s degree in history and literature from Harvard College in 1967.

While at Harvard, he was heavily involved in student theater, acting, and directing. He made his Harvard debut in Gilbert and Sullivan’s Utopia Limited. Likewise, he was awarded a Fulbright Scholarship to attend the London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art. Following graduation, he worked as the Director of the Arts and Literature Department at WBAI, the Pacifica radio station in New York City.

Who is the wife of John Lithgow? John Lithgow is married to Mary Yeager, a UCLA history professor. The couple gets married on December 12, 1981. Lithgow’s second wife is Mary. Nathan (son) and Phoebe (daughter) are the couple’s two children.

Jean Taynton, a former teacher, was his first love. On September 10, 1966, the couple married, but their marriage did not last long, and they divorced in 1980. Ian Lithgow (born 1972), their son, is also an actor. He has two grandchildren through his son Ian.

The 77-year-old actor is still physically fit and healthy. He stands 6 feet 3 inches tall and weighs approximately 78 kg. His eyes are blue, and he has grey hair.

How did John Lithgow start his Professional Career?

As a child, John made his acting debut in one of his father’s productions. Dealing: Or the Berkrlry-to-Boston Forty-Brick Lost-Bag Blues was his feature film debut in 1972. The following year, he made his Broadway debut in The Changing Room as a dull British rugby player.

He has appeared on Broadway in several productions since then, including Mt. Fat Friend (1974), A Memory of Teo Mondays (1976), Bedroom Farce (1979), Beyond Therapy (1982), and others. John Lithgow, who also played Lucas Sergeant in Bob Fosse’s autobiographical film, directed and choreographed All That Jazz.

He played Yoda in the NPR adaptations of The Empire Strikes Back and Return of the Jedi. Lithgow portrayed J. J. Hunsecker in the Martin Beck Theatre’s Broadway adaptation of the 1957 film Sweet Smell of Success in the early to mid-2000s. He starred in The Life and Death of Peter Sellers, a film based on the life of comedian Peter Sellers, in 2004.

Throughout his career, he has appeared in approximately forty television episodes and films. He had his first recurring role as Dick Solomon in the television series 3rd Rock from the Sun in 1996. He appeared on television shows such as ‘How I Met Your Mother,’ ‘Once Upon a Time in Wonderland (voice),’ ‘Drunk History,’ and ‘Twenty Good Years,’ among others.
