    Gillie Da Kid Career

    Da Kid was first thrown into the national limelight in 2006 from a dispute with Cash Money Records and its star rapper, Lil Wayne, when he argued to have ghost-written for some of the label’s artists, especially Wayne. Years prior to the embroilment, Gillie da Kid, real name Nasir Fard, and his Major Figgas clique were heavyweights in Philadelphia’s underground scene for pushing various independent records and mixtapes.

    The seven-person team lastly signed to the Houston, TX-based Suave House Records toward the end of the ’90s, but when the label lost its disbandment with Universal, Gillie went elsewhere to pursue a solo career. The boasting MC crossed paths with Cash Money CEO Bryan “Baby” Williams backstage at a concert in Philadelphia, and nearly within a week, he was signed to the New Orleans label.

    Due to disputes over his publishing, nonetheless, a solo record never occurred; relatively, he remained behind the scenes as a ghostwriter (though Cash Money still denies it). As did significant talents B.G., Juvenile, and Mannie Fresh prior to him, he exited Cash Money, while still remaining on good terms with Lil Wayne — that is, until 2006 when Wayne put out a set of unwarranted jabs against Gillie on one of his mixtapes.

    When Gillie let the cat out of the bag, the hip-hop press (both print and Internet) gave him tons of exposure. Mixtape gurus like DJ Kay Slay and DJ Drama were even seeking the rapper out to give new material. He thereafter inked a deal with rising indie label Babygrande Records, which issued The Best of the GDK Mixtapes compilation in March 2007.

    He released the single “Get Down on the Ground” in 2008, which would be shielded by Soulja Boy in 2013 as just “Get Down.”
