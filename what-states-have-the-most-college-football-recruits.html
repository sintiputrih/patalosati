What states have the most college football recruits?

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, September 30, 2024

When it comes to college football recruiting, certain states tend to produce more high school football players than others. According to recent data, Florida, Georgia, and Louisiana are among the top states for producing FBS (Football Bowl Subdivision) players per capita. However, when considering overall numbers, it is no surprise that Texas is the state that produces the most high school football players overall, including many highly recruited prospects.

In the 2015 class, SEC-affiliated states produced the most FBS players, with nine of the top 16 states in the per-capita rankings. These states include Alabama, Georgia, Louisiana, and the Carolinas. In more recent rankings, released by 247Sports for the class of 2023, Florida, Texas, California, and Georgia are among the top states for producing high-end talent.

While these rankings can give a general idea of which states produce the most college football recruits, it is important to note that they are based on a variety of factors and may not always be accurate. Coaches and recruiters must rely on their own evaluations and assessments to identify top prospects, and sometimes the most talented players may not have the highest star ratings or come from the most highly regarded states.

It is also worth noting that many college football programs recruit nationally and not just within their own state. In fact, some of the top recruits in recent years have come from states not traditionally known for producing high-end talent, such as Hawaii. Coaches and recruiters are often willing to take a chance on players who possess the talent, work ethic, and determination to succeed, regardless of their state of origin or star rating.

In conclusion, while certain states such as Florida, Georgia, and Louisiana tend to produce more college football recruits than others, recruiting rankings and per-capita statistics are not always accurate indicators of a player's potential. Coaches and recruiters must rely on their own evaluations and assessments to identify top prospects, and sometimes the most talented players may come from unexpected places.
