Lupillo Rivera Hospitalized for Emergency Surgery

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, February 15, 2024

The famous Mexican singer Lupillo Rivera had to be admitted to a hospital in Mexico City after suffering from severe abdominal pain. The brother of the late Jenni Rivera underwent an emergency surgery to remove his appendix, which was inflamed and could have caused serious complications.

What Happened to Lupillo Rivera?

According to Mazatl√°n Weekly, Lupillo Rivera was rushed to the hospital on Sunday, March 20, 2023, after experiencing a 24-hour episode of pain in his right lower abdomen. He had a similar condition a week before, which was treated as kidney stones in the United States.

At the hospital, he was diagnosed with acute appendicitis, a condition that occurs when the appendix becomes infected and swollen. The appendix is a small organ attached to the large intestine that has no known function. If left untreated, it can burst and cause a life-threatening infection.

Lupillo Rivera had to undergo a laparoscopic appendectomy, a minimally invasive surgery that involves making small incisions in the abdomen and using a camera and instruments to remove the appendix. The surgery was successful, but he had to stay in the hospital for observation and recovery.

How is Lupillo Rivera Doing Now?

According to ABC Noticias, Lupillo Rivera is doing well after the surgery, but he has to follow some hygienic and dietary measures, as well as rest for 10 days. This means that he had to cancel some of his upcoming concerts that he had scheduled in different parts of Mexico.

The singer apologized to his fans for the inconvenience and thanked them for their support and prayers. He also thanked the medical staff who attended him and assured that he will be back on stage as soon as possible.

Lupillo Rivera is one of the most popular and successful singers of regional Mexican music. He has released more than 20 albums and won several awards, including a Grammy in 2010. He is also known for being the brother of Jenni Rivera, who died in a plane crash in 2012.
